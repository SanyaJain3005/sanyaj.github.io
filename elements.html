Consumer-Centric Product Innovation – Entry-Level Pampers Launch

Situation: Pampers lacked an entry-level product, limiting its appeal to cost-sensitive consumers. While conducting field research, I discovered that customers perceived Pampers as too expensive and were deterred by the lack of low-count packs.

Task: To address this gap, I proposed a new product line tailored to price-sensitive customers.

Action:
- Conducted a Consumer Survey: Gathered data on consumer preferences and purchasing barriers.
- Analyzed Competitors: Benchmarked pricing, packaging, and product offerings.
- Built a Business Case: Used survey results, market segmentation, and projected financial impact to pitch the idea to senior leadership.

Result: Leadership approved the project, recognizing its potential to increase market share. The initiative received widespread appreciation for its consumer-centric approach.

Learning:
- Empathizing with consumers can uncover untapped opportunities.
- Data-driven insights are critical to securing buy-in.
- Innovating for underserved segments builds brand loyalty.

P&G Healthcare Product Recall – Regulatory Crisis Management

Situation: A technical glitch caused healthcare products intended for Sri Lanka to be deployed in India, creating a significant regulatory and consumer safety issue. The potential fallout included consumer health risks, regulatory fines of $250K–$1M, legal complications, and potential reputational damage, threatening a $6Bn business.

Task: Faced with two options—conducting an internal root cause analysis and resolving the glitch (a short-term fix with potential long-term consequences) or initiating a product recall (an immediate $1.1M cost)—I chose to prioritize consumer safety and brand reputation by executing the recall.

Action:
- Developed a Recall Strategy: Identified affected batches, mapped impacted regions, and formulated an actionable plan.
- Gained Stakeholder Buy-In: Leveraged data from brand perception surveys, consumer health risk analysis, and financial impact projections to secure leadership approval.
- Executed the Recall: Coordinated with distributors and external partners to retrieve, track, and quarantine products efficiently.

Result: Successfully recalled the products before they reached consumers, ensuring zero health incidents or regulatory penalties. Leadership commended my decisive action and consumer-first approach.

Learning:
- Always prioritize consumer well-being.
- Use data to guide decision-making and influence stakeholders.
- Adopt a long-term perspective over short-term gains.

Operational Turnaround – Team Efficiency Optimization

Situation: Upon joining P&G, I discovered that my 22-member team was operating at 73% efficiency—far below the expected standard.

Task: To enhance operational efficiency, I created a loss elimination plan. However, my initial approach of top-down delegation failed to yield results.

Action:
- Identified the Root Cause: Realized the importance of team collaboration and alignment.
- Engaged the Team: Held individual meetings to understand team members’ strengths, motivations, and challenges.
- Co-Created Solutions: Collaboratively redesigned the operational plan, incorporating inputs from the team to ensure ownership and accountability.

Result: Within six months, my team became the best-performing team in the plant, achieving 90% efficiency—a 17% improvement.

Learning:
- Inclusivity and collaboration drive better outcomes.
- Invest in team growth to foster accountability and engagement.
- Leadership is about empowering others, not just delegating tasks.

Leadership in Crisis – Sri Lanka Bankruptcy

Situation: During Sri Lanka’s economic crisis, distributors lacked foreign currency to pay P&G, jeopardizing a $60M business.

Task: To sustain operations, I had to devise an unconventional solution while mitigating financial risks.

Action:
- Conducted a Brainstorming Session: Collaborated with distributors to identify feasible solutions.
- Selected and Assessed a Strategy: Proposed leveraging distributors’ export revenues from other regions like the Maldives to settle payments, requiring extended payment terms from 30 to 60 days.
- Gained Leadership Approval: Presented a comprehensive risk assessment and financial implications to leadership, securing their support.

Result: Sustained operations in Sri Lanka for 8-9 months, safeguarding the $60M business and maintaining market presence until the situation stabilized.

Learning:
- Flexibility and innovation are critical in uncharted territory.
- Collaboration fosters resilient solutions.
- Strategic thinking can mitigate risks in volatile environments.

Digitization for Efficiency – Automating Leadership Reporting

Situation: Preparing a weekly leadership report took 4 hours due to data inconsistencies and manual workflows.

Task: To enhance efficiency, I automated the process to ensure timely and accurate reporting.

Action:
- Automated Data Workflows: Used KNIME to clean and structure raw data.
- Created Dynamic Dashboards: Leveraged Power BI to visualize data for leadership in an actionable format.

Result: Reduced reporting time from 4 hours to 30 minutes, significantly boosting productivity and ensuring real-time insights for decision-making.

Learning:
- Embrace technology to optimize processes.
- Invest time in learning new tools for long-term impact.
- Simplifying workflows enhances efficiency and accuracy.
